# Psalm 113–117: The Hallel Praise

Psalms 113 to 118 are collectively called the "Egyptian Hallel" (Hallel = Praise).
Significance:
Historical: These psalms were sung by the Jews during the Passover feast to remember God’s deliverance from Egypt (Exodus).
Liturgical: Traditionally, Psalms 113–114 were sung before the Passover meal, and Psalms 115–118 after the meal.
Jesus Connection: On the night He was betrayed, Jesus sang these Hallel psalms with His disciples (Matthew 26:30). This was likely the last song Jesus sang before going to the cross.

Overall Message of Hallel Psalms:

God is holy, powerful, and merciful.
He delivers His people from bondage.
He is worthy of eternal praise.

These psalms are full of joyful thanksgiving and Messianic hope — pointing directly to Jesus Christ as the ultimate Deliverer.
